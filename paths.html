The PATHS Program

PATHS is an NSF-funded training program that prepares autistic and neurodivergent adults for careers in the semiconductor industry, combining VR training, UF cleanroom experience, and one-on-one industry mentorship.

Visit the official PATHS site
Why PATHS

A strengths-first pipeline

The semiconductor industry has a talent gap, and quality assurance and inspection roles demand exactly the kind of sequential, detail-driven, visual-pattern work in which many autistic individuals excel — yet this population remains largely overlooked in hiring. PATHS closes that gap from both sides: rigorous technical training for participants, and a prepared, supported pipeline for employers.

The program is led by Dr. Hyo Kang with Dr. Navid Asadi and Dr. Nigel Newbutt, in partnership with UF's Center for Autism and Related Disabilities, the Digital Worlds Institute, the Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering, and the College of Education.

How it works

Eight weeks, step by step

  1. 1

    Orientation at UF — Week 1

    Participants travel to Gainesville (fully funded) to meet their cohort and train in person, including time in UF's cleanroom and the Security & Assurance (SCAN) Lab's advanced inspection facilities.

  2. 2

    Online + VR training — Weeks 2–7

    Lectures from nationally recognized scientists delivered through UF Canvas, paired with a provided VR headset for hands-on semiconductor visualization and practice in a controlled environment.

  3. 3

    Employment bootcamp — Week 8

    Resume and interview preparation plus one-on-one mentorship from industry experts at companies including Micron, AMD, Intel, and GlobalFoundries.

  4. 4

    Internship & placement

    Selected graduates move into funded internships with partners such as NextGen Semiconductor, NHanced, SkyWater, and Lockheed Martin — with stipends of up to $10,000 and the goal of converting internships into full-time roles.

Eligibility

Who should apply

Adults 18+ with an associate's or bachelor's degree in a STEM field who are U.S. citizens or permanent residents. Neurodivergent individuals are encouraged to apply.

Schedule

When it runs

The next cohort runs mid-September to mid-November 2026 in a hybrid format: one funded week in person at UF, the remainder online with VR. The Spring 2026 inaugural cohort trained 16 students from across the nation.

Outcomes

What you leave with

Hands-on cleanroom and inspection-tool experience, industry mentorship, a completion bonus, and a funded pathway to internships and full-time semiconductor careers.
